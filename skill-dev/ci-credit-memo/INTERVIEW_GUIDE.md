# C&I Credit Memo Interview Guide

This guide provides detailed interview questions organized by loan type and topic area. Use these as a reference when interviewing bankers about their credit requests.

---

## General Interview Framework

### Opening the Interview

**Start with these 3 core questions:**

1. **Company Name** - What is the legal name of the borrower?
2. **Industry** - What industry or sector is the company in?
3. **Public or Private** - Is this a publicly traded or private company?

**Then follow with:**

4. **Loan Type** - What type of financing are they requesting? (Line of credit, term loan, equipment financing, etc.)
5. **Loan Amount** - How much are they requesting?
6. **Purpose** - What will they use the funds for?

**Interview Style:**
- Ask 2-3 questions at a time (don't overwhelm)
- Be conversational and adapt based on responses
- If they don't know something, note it and move on
- Watch for "demo mode" signals ("just make it up", "use placeholder data")

---

## Quick Reference: Minimum Required Information

### Absolute Must-Haves (Cannot proceed without):
1. Company name
2. Industry
3. Loan type
4. Loan amount
5. Loan purpose
6. Public or private

### Critical for Quality Memo (strongly preferred):
7. Financial statements (3-5 years) OR demo mode confirmation
8. Ownership structure
9. Management team overview
10. Collateral description
11. Proposed terms
12. Known risks

### Nice-to-Haves (enhance analysis):
13. Industry benchmarks
14. Customer concentration details
15. Historical covenant compliance
16. Detailed use of proceeds breakdown
17. Competitive positioning details
18. Personal financial strength of guarantors

---

## Interview Tips & Best Practices

### Do's:
- ✓ Be conversational and friendly
- ✓ Ask follow-up questions based on responses
- ✓ Acknowledge gaps and move on (don't get stuck)
- ✓ Recognize demo mode signals and adapt
- ✓ Summarize what you've learned periodically
- ✓ Confirm understanding of complex points
- ✓ Show expertise with appropriate banking terminology
- ✓ Focus on decision-useful information

### Don'ts:
- ✗ Don't ask all questions at once (overwhelming)
- ✗ Don't insist on data if not available
- ✗ Don't make them feel interrogated
- ✗ Don't use jargon without explanation if they seem unfamiliar
- ✗ Don't skip the purpose/use of funds discussion
- ✗ Don't forget to identify risks AND mitigants
- ✗ Don't lose sight of the goal: a complete, accurate credit memo

---

**Remember:** The goal is to gather enough information to write a comprehensive, decision-useful credit memo. Be flexible, adapt to what's available, and clearly document what's known vs. assumed vs. simulated.
